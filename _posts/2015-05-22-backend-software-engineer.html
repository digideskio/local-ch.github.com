Backend Developer

The backend team develops the infrastructure to import, process, and store the phonebook- and advertisement data for Switzerland’s official directory. We are providing a highly optimized and reliable layer of delivery services to make this data accessible to our search engine and the web/mobile platforms. Our main challenge is to constantly push our technology stack forward and to be ready for next-gen features.

As a member of the backend team, you will be part of a small but highly committed group of developers that is collaborating very closely with web- and mobile engineering teams to provide the best solutions possible to our users and customers.

Roles and Responsibilities

  • You translate business requirements into technical solutions
  • You develop and improve our application architecture
  • You are responsible for all phases of the application life cycle
  • You work in a team, but are also able to execute tasks autonomously
  • You do code reviews and improve our code quality

Desired Skills & Experience

  • You are versatile and always eager to tackle new problems
  • You are used to agile working methodologies, SCRUM, XP, Kanban
  • You have professional development experience with Java, version 7+
  • You have profound knowledge of the Spring framework 4.0+ (mvc, data, security, batch, boot)
  • You have practical understanding of RESTful API design
  • You have experience with MongoDB, PostgreSQL and Redis
  • You have experience with Maven or Gradle
  • You have experience with GWT is a plus
  • You are comfortable in a Unix environment and on the command-prompt
  • You have basic scripting / automation skills
  • You have confidence in using Git
  • You have good habits of writing tests
  • You are fluent in English, German/French is a plus
  • You like being awesome!

Apply Today!

Simply send your CV and a link to your GitHub account or code examples to hrz@local.ch. Note: This position requires that candidates are legal to work and live in Switzerland.

Questions?

Questions about the job can be sent directly to the engineering team lead, Yannick Suter.
