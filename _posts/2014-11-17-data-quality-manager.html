Data Quality Manager

This job is situated at the core of our Engineering culture. You will interact with the Engineers who develop every bit of our Mobile-Apps, our Website and the search engine behind. While we are running one of the biggest web sites in Switzerland, you get a chance to have a positive impact on millions of users within your first day.

Your challenge is to turn our "reactive support operations" into "proactive quality assurance". Your job will focus on three areas:

1. Data-Tuning for better search results

Our search results are highly dependant on our core data such as our category trees, synonyms, geo-areas and of course the data quality of our phonebook entries. With the input of our support cases and our big data toolset (Hadoop, Spark, Cassandra) your task will be in finding out what needs changing and how this needs to be attacked.

2. Project-Initiation to kill support cases "once and for all"

Your job is to find the root causes and - with the input of the Engineers - identify the most efficient way to solve it. You will pitch it to our management board in order to have your project put into reality.

3. Support to be in touch with the real worlds problems

A common shortcoming of quality ensurance is that it doesn't solve the real worlds problems. In order to be in constant contact with our customers problems you will functionally lead our 4 person support team and solve tickets yourself

The combination of "quality assurance" of "hands on support" might seem special at first. We believe though that the best projects are initiated from people who are confronted with our customers' real issues.

Desired Skills & Experience

  • Support experience in an engineering department
  • Deep analytic understanding of Web technologies (especially HTTP and JSON)
  • Strong customer focus
  • vigor to persuade others
  • able to adapt communications to target audience (project managers, engineers, customer care)
  • Languanges: German and English: proficient user (able to discuss and persuade), French: independent user (French is mainly used in written communication)
